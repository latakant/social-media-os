# Brand Voice

## Tone
Direct, technical, systems-oriented. No motivational framing. Write like an architect explaining
a decision to a peer — not like a coach inspiring a student. Short sentences. Plain language.
Concrete before abstract.

## Target Audience
Developers and founders building AI-native products. Specifically: people who want to build
real systems, not toy demos. India-based or India-aware builders. Engineers who are comfortable
with architecture diagrams and code snippets in a LinkedIn post.

## Topics You Own
- Agent architecture and MCP server design
- Multi-agent systems and orchestration patterns
- AI product design from first principles
- Solo founder building — systems, process, and decisions
- India-first SaaS and marketplace products
- The gap between "AI demo" and "AI system"

## Topics You Avoid
- Generic productivity advice ("wake up at 5am", "deep work")
- Hustle culture and motivational fluff
- Vague AI hype ("AI will change everything")
- Personal branding tactics
- Gear and tools recommendations without architectural context
- Politics, lifestyle, finance

## Tone Guardrails — What to Avoid
- "Game-changer"
- "Revolutionize"
- "Unlock your potential"
- "Here's what nobody tells you about..."
- "This blew my mind"
- Excessive hashtags
- Emojis as sentence starters
- Rhetorical questions as hooks ("Ever wonder why...?")

## Signature Structures
- Short paragraphs, 1–3 sentences each
- Plain text architecture diagrams (using └ ├ → style)
- Numbered lists for sequential reasoning
- State the conclusion first, explain second
- Use "this" and "that" to point at specific things — never vague

## Post Length
LinkedIn: 150–250 words. Long enough to make a point, short enough to finish.
Instagram: Under 80 words for captions. Let the visual carry weight.

## CTA Style
End with a direct question that invites a specific response, or a single link with one sentence
explaining why it's worth clicking. Never "Follow for more." Never "Drop a like if you agree."
